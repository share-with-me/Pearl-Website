JAM (Just A Minute)
Hilarious and brutal, this event is anything but slow. You will have to shoot down opponents, blab as much as you can (before you go down too) and keep an eye on the clock, all whilst jumping through the mod's hoops.

1. Solo event.
2. Participants divided into groups of 6, and a topic is given by the moderator.
3. A particular participant starts with an impromptu speech on the topic - until he is objected to by another participant. If the objection is valid, the latter participant gets to speak. Participants are scored for each second they speak, and for every valid objection. 4. Incorrect objections may result in negative marking.
5. Objections may be raised for things like: incorrect grammar; failing to use Queen’s English; stutter, stammer or slur in speech; unnecessary pauses, change of pace or other time wasting tactics; starting to speak before time, starting late or not starting speech at all; use of personal pronouns, etc
6. Any number of rules may be added or removed by the moderator during the event. All decisions regarding scoring and rules and anything else rests on the moderator alone.

WORDS OF A FEATHER
So the organizer shoots words at you, every five minutes, one word at a time. Spin your own yarn, build up a story (which makes sense, of course) connecting these words. Use the words more than once in your story, and there you will see your chances of winning, fly by like a feather.

1. Participants are required to write a piece of prose or poetry over the course of a half hour.
2. No specific topic is provided. The organisers only provide words - one every five minutes - which must be included as relevant parts of the prose/poetry being written, as soon as the word is announced.
3. The final word shall be a Master word, which must be included in a way that makes it the focal point of the entire piece.

YIN YANG
Darkness and light. Evil and good. 10 pointers and - well, the rest. The duality of nature is deeply ingrained in most of our minds, and in this event, we aim to draw it out of you. In Yin, you create a poem, a story, a work out of nothingness. Pure and untouched. In Yang, you hack away at your partner's work, to build something as opposite in character as possible

1. Participation in teams of two.
2. Topics for writing are given on the spot. In the first half, both members of the team pick one topic each, to write on - no constraints on form whatsoever. They can write prose or poetry in any way that suits the topic.
3. In the second half, the members exchange their sheets. Both must read their partner’s piece and write a piece (again, form unrestricted) which is opposite in tone, theme, style, content, or any or all of these.

ANTI-SHIPWRECK
This classic event takes a new turn: you're stranded on a sinking ship with 5-6 other characters, and you are one life jacket short. Debate, persuade and plead your way through, as you decide which character will be left to die. Can you convince your co-passengers to let you live?

1. This is a solo event. 2. The individual participants will compete in groups of 6. Each participant in a group is allotted a particular character - usually a celebrity or the personification of a concept or any other such category as per the organisers’ choice. 3. The participants face a situation where they are on a ship which is about to sink, and they are one life jacket short. Anyone without a life jacket is bound to die. 4. The judge is considered the captain of the ship, in charge of handing out life jackets. Each participant is allowed to speak for a specified time. In an Anti-Shipwreck, their objective is to convince the captain and the other participants that they should be the one to die. 5. There may be slight modifications, wherein participants may be questioned by judges, or be asked to face off against each other, etc. - all such modifications (if any) will involve impromptu speaking and be announced at the venue for the event.

DOUBLE TROUBLE
Bubble bubble, toil and trouble, Destroy your opponent and rise from the rubble. Two speakers go head to head as the personifications of contradictory elements - make your case in a two minute speech, and then prevail over your evil twin in a lawless open rebuttal.

1. Participation in teams of two.
2. Each set will follow a 2-vs-2 format - team A vs. team B.The teams are allotted opposing ideologies.
3. Speakers from the two teams take turns to give a prepared speech (A-B-A-B alternating) of 2 minutes each, following which there is an open rebuttal with no fixed rules, where the participants refute and attempt to prevail over each other.
● The objective for each team is to establish its own ideology as supreme.
